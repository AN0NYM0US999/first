A Report on Megalodon


What is Megalodon?

Megalodon, meaning "big tooth", is an extinct species of shark that lived approximately 2.3 to 3.6 million years ago, during the Early Miocene to the Pliocene. It was formerly thought to be member of the family Lamnidae and a close relative of the great white shark.

What would have happened if Megalodon was alive?

Ans- Well?
What if it was? You see, such gigantic creatures prefer cold climates and as such megalodon would only exist around the poles. It would most definitely be the apex predator in its habitat and threaten or outright put extinct several smaller shark species (specially the great white). Other than that, it would have little (if any) impact on deep ocean ecosystems.
